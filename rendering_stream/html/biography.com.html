U.S. President

George Washington

George Washington, a Founding Father of the United States, led the Continental Army to victory in the Revolutionary War and was America’s first president.

Writer

Gwendolyn Brooks

Gwendolyn Brooks was a postwar poet best known as the first African American to win a Pulitzer Prize for her 1949 book 'Annie Allen.'

Musician

Selena Quintanilla

Known as the 'Queen of Tejano Music,' Selena Quintanilla was a beloved Latin recording artist who was killed by the president of her fan club at the age of 23.

Inventor

Alexander Graham Bell

Alexander Graham Bell was one of the primary inventors of the telephone, did important work in communication for the deaf and held more than 18 patents.

Amanda Knox

American college student Amanda Knox was convicted and then acquitted in the murder of her British roommate Meredith Kercher in Italy. Knox's acquittal was overturned in 2013 and she was again convicted of murder in 2014. Her conviction was overturned in 2015.

Jay-Z

In addition to his award-winning hip-hop albums, Jay-Z is known for an array of successful business interests, as well as his marriage to singer Beyoncé.

Julianne Moore

Acclaimed actress Julianne Moore has starred in a range of projects that include 'Boogie Nights,' 'The Hours,' 'Game Change' and 'Still Alice,' for which she won an Oscar in 2015.

Tyra Banks

A former supermodel, Tyra Banks turned her runway success into a multimedia brand and worked at the helm of two successful TV series simultaneously, 'America's Next Top Model,' and 'The Tyra Banks Show.'

Ozzy Osbourne

British musician Ozzy Osbourne fronted the heavy metal band Black Sabbath before embarking on a successful solo career. He later became a reality TV star with 'The Osbournes.'

Ryan Reynolds

Canadian actor Ryan Reynolds is mainly known for his comedic performances, particularly as the star of the superhero franchise 'Deadpool.'
