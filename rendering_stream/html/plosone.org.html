Accelerating the publication of peer-reviewed science

Read the latest COVID-19 research

This Collection highlights content published across the PLOS journals relating to the COVID-19 pandemic.

Read More

11/17/2020

Climate Science

Persistent heat waves projected for Middle East and North Africa by the end of the 21st century

Varela and colleagues explain how heat waves may affect the Middle East and North Africa during the 21st century, and find that both the mean and maximum intensity of heat waves are likely to increase.

Image credit: pone.0242477

11/11/2020

Mental Health

Predictors of mental health during the Covid-19 pandemic in the US: Role of economic concerns, health worries and social distancing

Kämpfen and colleagues analyze online survey data relating to mental health during the COVID-19 pandemic. They find that concerns about the economic consequences of the pandemic were correlated to worsening mental health.

Image credit: Engin Akyurt, Pixabay

11/11/2020

Social Psychology

Labelling affects agreement with political statements of right-wing populist parties

In this pre-registered study, Neumann and colleagues investigate whether mere labelling of political statements as endorsed by a right-wing populist party influences people’s agreement with such statements.

Image credit: Maryland GovPics, Flickr

11/17/2020

Women's Health

Association between maternal satisfaction with breastfeeding and postpartum depression symptoms

de Avilla and colleagues demonstrate the importance of maternal satisfaction with breastfeeding and positive mental health outcomes in Brazillian mothers.

Image credit: Terri Cnudde, Pixabay

11/27/2020

EVERYONE BLOG

Growing together– celebrating tropical agriculture research in PLOS ONE

Senior Editor Jamie Males reflects on the pressures and demands faced by agriculture in the tropics,  highlighting recent PLOS ONE studies that showcase how researchers are rising to the challenge.

Image credit: Efraimstochter, Pixaby

11/16/2020

Cardiovascular Medicine

Factor analysis for the clustering of cardiometabolic risk factors and sedentary behavior, a cross-sectional study

Leu and colleages reports on the independent and unique impact of sedimentary behaviour on cardiovascular disease in a Taiwanese population.

Image credit: ArtCoreStudios, Pixabay

11/17/2020

Computational biology

A novel deep learning-based point-of-care diagnostic method for detecting Plasmodium falciparum with fluorescence digital microscopy

Holmström and colleagues developed a new deep learning method to detect and quantify parasites in thin blood smears

Image credit: pone.0242355

11/16/2020

Molecular Dynamics

Including residual contact information into replica-exchange MD simulations significantly enriches native-like conformations

Voronin and colleagues present a new method for simulating protein structure by including contact information from a contact-based sigmoidal bias potential.

Image credit: pone.0242477

11/20/2020

Pathogens

Prediction of B. pseudomallei DsbA substrates identifies potential virulence factors and vaccine targets

Vezina and colleagues conduct analysis of B. pseudomallei disease-associated strains and discover putative virulence factors that could serve as high priority targets for vaccine development.

Image credit: pone.0241306

11/11/2020

Psychology

Semantic knowledge influences visual working memory in adults and children

Starr and colleagues study whether semantic knowledge confers a benefit for visual working memory by using familiar and unfamiliar real-world objects.

Image credit: Wikimedia Commons

11/13/2020

Cell Biology

The Arf-GEF Steppke promotes F-actin accumulation, cell protrusions and tissue sealing during Drosophila dorsal closure

West and Harris investigate the role of Stepkke in actin polymerization at the epidermal leading edge during Drosophila embryo dorsal closure.

Image credit: pone.0239357
