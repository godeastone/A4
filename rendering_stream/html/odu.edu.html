Old Dominion University

  1. Welcome

    Welcome to Old Dominion University, where student success is at the heart of the Monarch experience. Our 250-acre campus in Norfolk, Virginia, is home to approximately 25,000 undergraduates, graduate students and international students. Our location in the coastal region of Virginia allows for serious study and play, and we're excited to share a bit about both with you during your virtual tour.

    Before we get started, let me explain the many ways you can explore our beautiful space.
    -You can click on the forward-facing arrow to start walking around. -You can jump to the next stop by clicking on the "Next" button.
    -If you already know where you want to go, you can select any location by using the list OR by clicking directly on the map.
    -Lastly, remember that you can explore any location in more detail by clicking the supplemental content.

  2. Webb University Center and Kaufman Mall

    The Webb University Center, ODU's student union, houses Career Development Services, the Center for Major Exploration, Student Health Services and several food outlets, meeting spaces and lounges. Just outside is Kaufman Mall, where you'll find our iconic, 6-foot-high lion fountain. The bronze Monarch, designed by alumnus and adjunct faculty member Kevin Gallup, sits atop a platform in the university fountain, where the water flows blue at homecoming and graduation. In the center of Kaufman Mall is the University Seal, commemorating our institutional founding. Students are warned not to tread upon it if they want to graduate. Kaufman Mall is also a favorite gathering spot for warm weather fun and tailgating.

  3. University Village and the Arts District

    Located just across Hampton Boulevard along Monarch Way is the University Village and ODU's Arts District. The "village" includes fully furnished 1-, 2- and 4-bedroom apartments for upperclassmen and transfer students. It also boasts several restaurants, coffeehouses, the University Village Bookstore, art galleries, the Goode Theatre and the Barry Arts Building. The arts building features studios, labs and the Hofheimer Art Library, with 10,000 volumes on architecture, sculpture, print media and more.

  4. Darden College of Education

    The Darden College of Education is nationally recognized for preparing students to be education, industry and clinical/service professionals. With five floors and 120,000 square feet, the building provides high-tech amenities, collaborative space, a two-story-high multipurpose auditorium, nine laboratories for STEM and industrial technology programs, and a resource room dedicated to student advising. Directly across Hampton Blvd from the Education building is the Ted Constant Convocation Center, home to ODU Basketball, concerts, lecture series, special events, freshman convocation and graduation. The Ted, an independently owned and run venue, is an area attraction we're lucky enough to have located on our campus.

  5. The Science Corner

    The "Science Corner" is home to our College of Sciences. The Oceanography and Physical Sciences building features the iconic OmniGlobe in its lobby; the globe's interactive surface displays various maps of the world. This area is also where you'll find our Pretlow Planetarium and Kaplan Orchid Conservatory. Beyond the campus, our faculty and students explore the waterways on our own research boat, the RV Fay Slover.

  6. Broderick Dining Commons

    ODU's Kate and John R. Broderick Dining Commons is a 39,000-square-foot state-of-the-art, Leadership in Energy and Environmental Design Gold Certified free-standing dining hall. This building was named for our president and first lady at the request of student leaders in recognition of their efforts spearheading inclusion and student success. With eight dining options, the "Commons" provides a restaurant-style environment while creating a strong sense of community for students, faculty and staff. Everything is made fresh on site, and meals are prepared to order. Options include: Bud's American Classics, Maize South American Kitchen, EVOO Italian Cuisine, Rice Sticks Asian Kitchen, Uno Mas Fresh Mexican Flavors, Mosaic International and 350 Degrees Baked Goods.

  7. ODU Athletic Complexes

    In 2009, we added football to our NCAA Division I athletic roster, and in 2013 the team joined Conference USA. Since we've welcomed the program back, the community, alums and students alike fill the S.B. Ballard Stadium seats! And in 2016, the team brought home its first bowl title, claiming victory in the Bahamas Bowl!

  8. Whitehurst Beach and The Sailing Center

    Whitehurst Hall consists of two six-story towers overlooking the Elizabeth River. This prime waterfront residence hall shares its Whitehurst Beach shoreline with ODU's Sailing Center – home to our award-winning sailing team. A favorite place for student organizations and individuals, the Beach is where you can catch a game of volleyball, challenge yourself on our newest low ropes course, grill a burger or string up your hammock and relax. Enjoy the sunset from these campus landmarks.

  9. Quad Residences and the SRC

    Five of ODU's residence halls are located on the quad. Most offer double or triple rooms with a shared bath. All quad residences are conveniently located near Webb Center, the P.O.D. market in Dominion House, the Broderick Dining Commons and the Student Recreation Center (SRC). The SRC boasts 15,000 square feet of exercise space, including basketball courts, group exercise classes, cross-fitness training, a 2-story rock-climbing wall and a swimming pool. You can also rent equipment like bikes and canoes or participate in one of our many intramural sports.

  10. Constant Hall and Strome Entrepreneurial Center

    Constant Hall serves as the hub for the Strome College of Business at Old Dominion University. In addition to the Greg Lumsden Trading Room, with its 24 Bloomberg terminals, Constant Hall boasts two full-sized lecture halls and 19 other classrooms equipped with powerful media and display capabilities. Adjacent to Constant Hall is the Strome Entrepreneurial Center, the result of an $11 million gift to ODU from the Strome Family Foundation. Since its opening, the center has helped provide an innovative, co-curricular program empowering ODU students - from all programs of study - to launch and expand their own businesses.

  11. Diehn Performing Arts Building

    ODU's College of Arts & Letters' Music Department and Dance Programs find their home in the Diehn Fine and Performing Arts Center. This impressive facility includes recital halls, dance studios, recording studios and a composer's library. Additionally, the F. Ludwig Diehn Composers Room provides students with services and resources related to musical collections held by ODU Libraries.

  12. Dragas Hall

    Dragas Hall, a multi-purpose facility, is home to the International Office of Admissions, the English Language Center and the Office of Study Abroad. Monarchs enjoy a number of varied and enriching opportunities to study abroad as part of their academic program. Approved courses taken overseas are fully transferrable to meet major, minor or general education requirements. Exclusive to incoming freshmen is our First Year Experience Abroad. Participating students travel to Greece with faculty members, and earn general education credits in just two weeks!

  13. Perry Library

    This four-story building contains more than 1.5 million print items and provides students access to a number of other resources, including 300 computers, an inter-library loan system and librarians for each academic college. The Learning Commons creates a collaborative environment allowing students to rearrange furniture and reserve rooms specified for group work and presentations. Students can also receive help from Student Support Services, located in the back of the library, with the Office of Educational Accessibility, the Military Connection Center, the Writing Center and free tutoring.

  14. College of Health Sciences

    ODU's College of Health Sciences is one of our most competitive academic colleges! Students in these programs are provided with the best training and hands-on experience possible through clinical requirements and volunteer opportunities with regional health care providers. With pass rates for licensure in the top percentiles in the nation, the College of Health Sciences has gained both a national and international reputation for its research and preparation of future health professionals.

  15. College of Engineering and the Systems Building

    ODU's engineering programs are housed in three buildings, Kaufman Hall, E.V. Williams Engineering & Computational Sciences Building and our newest addition, the Engineering Systems Building (ESB). The ESB's design reflects changes in engineering teaching methods that go well beyond the classroom. Increased collaboration space, biomedical engineering, laser and plasma labs are just a few of the building's assets. With innovative learning spaces and an abundance of research opportunities for students, it's no surprise ODU produces the second largest percentage of Science, Technology, Engineering, Math, and Health Sciences (STEM-H) graduates in the state of Virginia.
