Tell us a little about you

Tell us a little about you

  • Choose "Married" if you were married as of December 31, 2018.
  • Choose "Have kids" if you have a child who lived with you more than half the year and is under 19 (or under 24 and a full-time student) or permanently disabled. Also, choose this category if you are the primary source of financial support for a child or other relative, such as a parent. Note that dependency tests must be satisfied to qualify for a dependent-related tax benefit.
  • Choose "Own A home" if you owned a home at any time during 2018.

What about work?

What about work?

  • Choose "Freelancer/Contractor" if you provided services to one or more businesses and you receive a 1099 instead of a W-2. Some examples of this include consultants, designers, writers, etc.
  • Choose "Self Employed" if you received income for services you performed or products you sold and you were directly paid by your clients. Some examples of this would be an Uber/Lyft driver, selling on Etsy, etc. Typically, self-employed will receive a 1099-misc from business clients, but will have to provide other records of payments from individual consumers. If you received payments from a third-party, such as Etsy or Uber, you may receive a 1099-K in January or early February reporting payments you received in 2018.
  • Choose "Own a business" if you received income through a Partnership or S Corporation or you own an unincorporated business or farm.

Any other income?

Any other income?

  • Choose "Have retirement income" if you received distributions from a retirement account in 2018. You will typically receive a 1099-R from your account trustee reporting these distributions.
  • Choose "Own Stock/ Investments" if you received income from the sale or ownership of stocks, bonds, REITs, PTPs or other securities in 2018.
  • Choose "Own rentals" if you received income from a tenant for the use of a home you own or sub-leased. This would include being a host for AirB&B, VRBO, or any similar house sharing service.
  • Choose "Have other income" if you earn some income from a nonbusiness activity without the expectation of making a profit. This includes income from hobbies, casual gambling winnings, prizes from amateur competitions, etc. Do NOT choose this if you are an Uber/Lyft driver, receive rental income, or earnings from self-employment.
