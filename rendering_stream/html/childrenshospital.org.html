Important updates from Boston Children's about COVID-19

Boston Children’s Hospital is working closely with the Massachusetts Department of Public Health by following their protocol. Since mid-January, our leadership team has been meeting multiple times a week to prepare for this scenario and to ensure that all protocols are followed. We are working around the clock to ensure the safety of our patients and the entire community during this unprecedented time.

 COVID-19 information   What to expect during your visit  Virtual visits  Donate blood & ways to help 

If you have questions about COVID-19 and your upcoming appointment, please call your specialty clinic. If you are a Boston Children's patient or family member and you have questions general questions about COVID-19 please call our COVID-19 hotline at 855-281-5730 or 617-355-4200.

There is a voluntary recall of Narcan nasal spray emergency medical kits made by HealthFirst.com. Instead of two doses, the box contains only one dose of Narcan nasal spray. Please open the box and check to be sure you have two doses. If you have two doses, there is nothing more you need to do. If only one dose is in the box, please contact the pharmacy where you purchased it for a replacement.

There is a voluntary recall of Stimate Nasal Spray 1.5 mg/mL made by Ferring Pharmaceuticals. The amount of medication in the nasal spray is higher than noted on their specifications. If you currently have Stimate Nasal Spray, please call your pharmacy.

There is a voluntary recall certain lots of Desmopressin Acetate Nasal Spray 10 mcg/0.1mL, 5mL glass vial with spray cap made by Amring Pharmaceuticals. The amount of medication in the nasal spray is much higher than noted on their specifications and could result in serious injury when used. If you currently have Desmopressin Acetate Nasal Spray, please call your pharmacy to find out if it is one of the affected lots.
