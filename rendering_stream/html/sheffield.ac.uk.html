News

1 December 2020

Scanning technique reveals hidden damage to lungs from Covid-19

Early findings from our collaborative study into the longer term effects of Covid-19 suggest the use of cutting-edge scanning techniques may detect previously unseen lung damage.
24 November 2020

Next generation of autonomy inspired by insects

Our engineers are redefining how autonomous systems are built. Inspired by the brains of insects, new solutions for autonomy can mimic tasks such as seeing, sensing objects and navigating.
20 November 2020

Scientists and food historians collaborate to revive and grow extinct varieties of rice

Historians and plant scientists at our Institute for Sustainable Food are developing types of rice that can thrive in an increasingly extreme modern climate while delivering the high yields and nutrients required by an ever-expanding population.
18 November 2020

Mapping how Covid-19 spreads and evolves

Our researchers are working with partners in the Covid-19 Genomics UK Consortium to map how Covid-19 spreads and evolves. A £12.2 million funding boost will help increase genome sequencing capacity across the national network.
