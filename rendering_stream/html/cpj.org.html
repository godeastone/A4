Egypt uses trumped-up terrorism charges to prolong journalists’ detention

Photographer Sayed Abd Ellah and blogger Mohamed “Oxygen” Ibrahim remain in detention after their scheduled release dates due to last-minute terrorism charges filed by Egyptian authorities. Al-Manassa editor Nora Younis tells CPJ how the government uses a variety of charges--from copyright infringement to incitement--to stifle independent reporting. (Photo: Reuters/Mohamed Abd El Ghany)

CPJ calls on Bangladeshi prime minister to arrange for release of journalist Shafiqul Islam Kajol

October 1, 2020 10:00 AM EDT

CPJ asks Bangladeshi Prime Minister Sheikh Hasina to arrange for the immediate and unconditional release of jailed journalist Shafiqul Islam Kajol, whose ill health makes him especially vulnerable to COVID-19 infection, potentially putting his life in danger.
