Take a Virtual Tour

University of Houston

  1. University Drive

    Welcome to the University of Houston, a tier one public research institution. Established in 1927, UH is situated on nearly 500 hundred acres in the center of a sprawling metropolis. We operate dozens of research centers and institutes on campus empowering students to solve real-world problems. If you already know where you want to go, you can select any location by using the list or by clicking directly on the map. You can explore any location in more detail by clicking the interactive elements of a stop. Here on University Drive, enjoy a scenic walk through Lynn Eusan Park, visit the on-campus hotel, Hilton University of Houston, grab a book at the 8-story M.D. Anderson Library or stop by the student center.

  2. Student Center

    Learn how to get involved on campus at the Student Center - the hub of student life. Whether you're looking for coffee, dinner or a snack, the food court has a variety of options. You'll also find a wide selection of study and meeting rooms, lounge spaces, and a game room complete with bowling lanes and billiards. Get comfortable in the Student Center Theater, where you can see movies or events put on by one of over 500 student organizations. And, don't forget to visit the UH Campus Store to stock up on books, supplies, and of course some Cougar Red merchandise. At UH, we always wear Red on Fridays!

  3. Student Housing

    Whether you are a first-year student or graduate student, there are multiple on-campus student housing options focused on your success. Cougar Village 1 and Cougar Village 2 are open only to first-year students. Complete with a fitness room, computer lab and laundry room, get comfortable in your new home away from home! There are always plenty of activities to keep students engaged including games, karaoke and movie nights, along with glow in the dark bowling and billiards. There's 24/7 security in each residential hall, in addition to on-duty desk assistants and residential advisors.

  4. University Lofts

    Loft-style living doesn't get better than this. University Lofts is for students who have junior standing or higher or are age 21 or older by September 1st. It includes several study lounges, fitness center, free laundry facilities, wifi, a private courtyard, two-roof terrace with views of downtown Houston, and a sky lounge with outdoor seating. University Lofts houses a Cougar Xpress market and a Subway restaurant as well as a pool table, computer lab and special event kitchen for programs and activities. Enjoy the modern, urban look and feel of 10-foot ceilings, granite countertops, exposed concrete ceilings and floors. Furnished and unfurnished units are available with a refrigerator, stove, microwave and dishwasher in the 2-bedroom units.

  5. Dining Commons

    The University of Houston offers a broad array of international flavors, hometown favorites and modern conveniences for an A+ dining experience. Committed to sustainability, UH is home to multiple Green Restaurant Certified dining commons, including Cougar Woods. Up late studying? We have 24/7 options. We also have more than 30 retail dining brands that feature local Houston food vendors as well as national favorites such as Panda Express, Chick-fil-A and Starbucks, which offer state-of-the art mobile ordering and delivery. You'll also find one of the most vibrant food truck programs on any university campus. Always get the flavors and nutrition you want, when and where you need it!

  6. Recreation and Wellness Center

    Healthy mind, healthy body! Between classes, get moving at the Campus Recreation and Wellness Center. So much more than just a gym, this comprehensive facility features a 53-foot tall climbing wall, along with multiple courts for basketball, volleyball, racquetball, soccer and more. The Fitness Zone has all the weights and machines you need to build muscle or increase endurance. Workout or cool off in outdoor and indoor swimming pools. Take advantage of free group fitness classes, intramural sports or even outdoor adventure opportunities, like hiking. The Rec Center also offers lots of opportunities for an on-campus job, such as a lifeguard, fitness instructor, or desk attendant.

  7. Cullen Family Plaza Fountains

    Relax to the soothing sounds of water flowing at the Cullen Family Plaza Fountains in the heart of campus. This popular gathering place and study hub honors the Cullen family, whose tradition of giving to UH dates back to the 1930s. Situated between the Ezekiel Cullen and Roy Cullen Buildings, Farish Hall and Hoffman Hall, the water centerpiece serves as a popular photo backdrop throughout the year, especially on graduation day. UH tradition says rubbing the paw on the statue of our mascot, Shasta, brings good luck - whether you're taking a final exam or headed to the big game.

  8. M.D. Anderson Library

    University of Houston Libraries offer a variety of services and resources, including a multimedia studio, technology workshops, plenty of study space, and equipment for check-out. Here at M.D. Anderson Library you'll find three computer labs with printing options. Need to practice a presentation with your team? Reserve a group room online. Need research help? Our subject librarians are here to assist. UH Libraries is also home to a hidden gem known as Special Collections, featuring rare and unique archives in eleven collecting areas. The library system consists of four additional libraries on campus so you'll find everything you need for student success.

  9. TDECU Stadium

    Cheer on the Cougars at TDECU Stadium -- home of Houston Football. The 40,000-seat venue features an exterior of red metal and aluminum panels that allow for optimal air flow and natural lighting into the concourse areas. There's not a bad seat in the house -- especially on the open-air lower bowl party decks. Stadium plazas pay tribute to UH history and the proud tradition of Houston Football. Sitting adjacent to the stadium is the Indoor Practice Facility. Not only does the team practice there, but fans can enjoy climate-controlled tailgating on gamedays!

  10. Fertitta Center

    Named after globally recognized entrepreneur Tilman Fertitta, the Fertitta Center is open for all men's and women's basketball home games and women's volleyball games. Your heart will pound with excitement inside the modern 7,035-seat sports and entertainment facility, unveiled in 2018. With abundant premium seating available, you can enjoy the action from the comfort of a leather recliner! You can also catch a concert or other fun events, including UH commencement ceremonies, here. Near the Fertitta Center you'll find the Guy V. Lewis Development Facility, providing resources to the men's and women's basketball student-athletes to excel throughout the season in all areas.

  11. Athletics/Alumni Center

    The Athletics/Alumni Center is one of the finest collegiate athletics facilities in the country, home to a world-class banked indoor track where the Cougars run for gold. Student-athletes from all sports use the center to practice, compete, work out, rehabilitate, study, meet and relax. Amenities include locker rooms and a 16,500-square foot weight room along with the Dominic and Ellen Ng Academic Center for Excellence. The UH Alumni Association offices are also housed here to support the hundreds of thousands of Cougar alumni around the city and country.

  12. Wilhelmina's Grove

    Wilhelmina's Grove is at the center of the UH Arts District, connecting the venues associated with the Kathrine G. McGovern College of the Arts, along with the Valenti School of Communication. An abundance of trees characterizes the Grove's serene setting, while concrete seating areas offer spaces for socializing and studying. At night, hanging lanterns transform the space into a perfect pavilion to host events before and after plays and concerts. Also keep your eye out for temporary public art exhibitions here. The UH System is home to the one of the largest public art collections in the country, featuring internationally renowned artists and over 700 pieces.

  13. Moores Opera House

    The 800-seat Moores Opera House is arguably the finest university opera production facility in the country. Opened in 1997, it was designed to emulate the acoustic space of old European opera houses while providing the modern equipment demanded in today's world. Boasting an enormous orchestra pit and full fly capacity, it allows performances of the largest operas while maintaining a sense of intimacy. The flexible-size orchestra shell transforms the performance space to highlight the Kathrine G. McGovern College of the Arts orchestras, wind ensembles, choral groups, and chamber music. Look up and discover the popular art installation "Euphonia" by famed artist Frank Stella that attracts visitors from around the globe.

  14. AD Bruce Religion Center

    The A.D. Bruce Religion Center is the focal point for many on-campus religious and spiritual activities, offering the campus community a place for both quiet meditation and spiritual growth. It's also a bustling center of many ongoing educational programs and activities focusing on religious issues with an emphasis on Interfaith Dialogue. The Religion Center has two chapels, The University Chapel and the smaller Meditation Chapel which are available for weekend events. The University Chapel is a popular venue for weddings, quinceañeras, baptisms, funerals & memorials, recitals, and lectures. Over 200 weddings are conducted here each year.

  15. Downtown Houston

    Downtown Houston is less than two miles away from the university and a Metro light rail line runs through campus making it quick and easy to see the sights and sounds of this booming metropolis. Catch an Astros or Rockets game, grab dinner at countless restaurants, take in a show or visit the popular park known as Discovery Green. In addition, Metro bus routes connect campus to a network of transit centers providing service across the Houston metro area. In an effort to better serve the ever-growing campus community, UH offers incentives for those who carpool or use alternative and sustainable transportations options.

  16. Technology Bridge

    The UH Technology Bridge is a rising innovation park suited for startups, large research laboratories, pilot scale facilities and light manufacturing. The 74-acre site across Spur 5 from the university is an ideal location for early stage and established companies looking to join a university ecosystem and boost local economic development. The UH Innovation Center and Incubator Labs provide office space and lab facilities to move concepts and ideas closer to the market. As a hub of entrepreneurial activity, it's also a great place for students to find employment or start a business of their own. Hundreds of student businesses and dozens of faculty startups have launched from UH in recent years.

  17. UH at Sugar Land

    The University of Houston at Sugar Land is an instructional site offering undergraduate, graduate and professional certificate opportunities. Think of it as one university, two zip codes. The campus spans 270 acres, located at US-59 south and University Boulevard, near the Brazos River in Fort Bend County. UH at Sugar Land is home to two inaugural programs in nursing and digital media. The College of Technology has a dedicated building here too. You'll find large state-of-the-art classrooms, computer and multimedia laboratories, as well as a campus library and bookstore. A free shuttle service is available for students who take classes at both the UH campus and at this instructional site.

  18. UH at Katy

    The University of Houston at Katy brings tier one higher education to a growing region, rich in ambition, drive and vision. Opened in 2019, the facility features state-of-the-art labs and studios for students pursuing studies with the UH College of Nursing and the UH Cullen College of Engineering, as well as the University of Houston-Victoria at Katy. Located near the intersection of I-10 and the Grand Parkway, the 46 acres of the Katy instructional site represent support and partnership of the region. Aspiring nurses can get their traditional Bachelor of Science in Nursing degree here while future global engineers can take advantage of the innovative subsea engineering program.

  • Personality Changes Predict Early Career Outcomes

    A new study by a University of Houston psychologist may hold the key to job success. It finds young people who develop higher levels of conscientiousness and emotional stability during the transition to employment tend to be more successful in some aspects of their early careers.

  • COVID-19, Economy Top Concerns for Texas Voters

    With less than two months before the Legislature convenes, a new survey from the Hobby School of Public Affairs found that Texans overwhelmingly say the COVID-19 pandemic and the need for economic growth are their top concerns.

  • Discoveries Highlight New Possibilities for Magnesium Batteries

    Researchers from the University of Houston and the Toyota Research Institute of North America have reported a breakthrough in the development of magnesium batteries, allowing them to operate at room temperature and deliver a power density comparable to that of lithium-ion batteries.

  • Misinformation or Artifact: A New Way to Think about Machine Learning

    Machine learning has delivered amazing results, but there also have been failures, ranging from the harmless to potentially deadly. New work from UH philosopher Cameron Buckner suggests that common assumptions about the cause behind these supposed malfunctions may be mistaken, information that is crucial for evaluating the reliability of these networks.

  • Minimizing the Impact of Restaurant Shutdowns, Restrictions in China Amid COVID-19 Crisis

    A new study led by the UH Conrad N. Hilton College of Hotel and Restaurant Management identifies aspects of restaurant operations in China that benefitted the bottom line despite the turmoil caused by the COVID-19 pandemic.

  • UH College of Technology Receives Transformative Gift from Sugar Land’s HCSS

    The University of Houston College of Technology is the recipient of a nearly $2 million gift to endow scholarships and support new infrastructure in its University of Houston at Sugar Land building. The gift generously comes from HCSS, a Sugar Land innovative software company for the construction industry.

  • New Drug Discovery Institute Launches at University of Houston

    The University of Houston has launched the Drug Discovery Institute (DDI) aimed at integrating new technologies, such as artificial intelligence, to streamline and modernize the drug-discovery process. With an emphasis on multi-disciplinary collaborations to include the Texas Medical Center among others, the DDI is going beyond the life and physical sciences to develop novel drugs for unmet medical needs, thus defining a new paradigm in academic drug discovery.

  • UH Wolff Center Again Ranked No. 1 in Entrepreneurship by the Princeton Review

    The Cyvia and Melvyn Wolff Center for Entrepreneurship within the C.T. Bauer College of Business has been named the No. 1 undergraduate entrepreneurship program in the United States. It also ranked No. 1 in 2019.

  • A Vision for Thriving, Sustainable Global Communities

    "City of Refugees,” an exhibition by the University of Houston Gerald D. Hines College of Architecture and Design is on view at the Aedes Architecture Forum in Berlin, Germany. The interactive show is in tandem with “Purgatory,” an art installation by world-renowned Chinese activist and artist Ai Weiwei.

  • Job Interest Not a Big Predictor of Job Satisfaction

    Interest in an occupation matters, but not as much as you might think when it comes to job satisfaction. While it's not a strong predictor of satisfaction, a University of Houston researcher found that it may help in your performance on the job.

  • Two Genes Regulate Social Dominance

    Using the Nobel Prize gene-editing technique, a University of Houston researcher has found that two genes regulate social dominance in cichlid fish and - possibly - humans.

  • UH Community Health Workers Join City of Houston in COVID-19 Fight

    A team of community health workers, trained by the University of Houston and embedded in some of Houston’s most vulnerable neighborhoods, has joined the city’s new COVID-19 Community Health Education Fellows program to combat COVID-19.
