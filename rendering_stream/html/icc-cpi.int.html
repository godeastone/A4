To participate in containing the COVID-19 spread and in line with the measures adopted by the Host State, the ICC adopted measures to ensure business continuity and fulfilment of its mandate while protecting the personnel and their families.

The Court has resumed judicial hearings. Access to the Court headquarters for public hearings is by appointment only, please check our Visit Us page for more information.

Other group visits are suspended until further notice.

The ICC is currently hearing 7 cases: the Abd-Al-Rahman case, and the Gicheru and Bett case (pre-trial), the Al-Hassan case, the Yekatom and Ngaïssona case, and the Ongwen case (before Trial Chambers), the Ntaganda case, and the Gbagbo and Blé Goudé case (on appeal).

ICC is using its web streaming service to broadcast the hearings with a 30 minute delay.
