    Liberty University

    1. Academic Lawn

      Welcome to Liberty University, a private, Christ-centered university located in Central Virginia near the Blue Ridge Mountains. My name is Nora, and I will be your tour guide today as we explore our campus spanning more than 7,000 acres. To begin your tour, click the front facing arrow and at any point during the tour just click the next button to go forward or the backwards facing arrow to see a stop again. There are also supplemental photos and videos throughout the tour so be sure and spend time exploring each of those, now let's get started!

    2. Vines Center

      The Vines Center is a large arena with more than 10,000 seats and serves as home to numerous NCAA Div. I athletic teams, as well as the venue for campus events such as concerts, movie nights, and a student talent show called Coffeehouse. You'll spend time here each Wednesday and Friday morning at Convocation, a unique opportunity for the campus community to come together to worship and hear from world-renowned speakers. We've had Francis Chan, Brett Favre, and Candace Cameron Bure (pronounced "burr-ray") come and share life lessons from diverse perspectives with our student body.

    3. DeMoss Hall Academic Programs

      DeMoss (pronounced "di-moss") Hall is our primary academic building and houses multiple departments as well as the majority of general education classes. Its four stories are filled with optimized technology and recent renovations, but most importantly with professors who genuinely invest in and care about the academic and personal successes of their students. If you're planning on studying in the School of Nursing, Education, Aeronautics, or Engineering or in the College of Applied Studies & Academic Success (CASAS) or Arts & Sciences, this is where your classes will mostly be held. With a 24:1 student to teacher ratio, you'll develop strong relationships with these seasoned professionals to launch your career.

    4. Green Hall Academics

      The Marie F. Green Hall not only is home to multiple dining options, our campus gym and recreation space, and the Tower Theatre but also classrooms, studios, and labs for multiple academic programs. Studying in the School of Communications & the Arts? Our RED Weapon cameras, art and broadcasting studios, and editing and design labs are found here. If you're interested in pursuing law, Liberty's School of Law lives here, with its own library and mock Moot Court room. Additionally, the Helms (pronounced "helm-s") School of Government holds its classes in Green Hall, where students can learn from faculty members who have worked for agencies like Homeland Security and the FBI. Lastly, this academic facility also hosts the Office of Equity and Inclusion, which promotes inclusiveness and impartiality throughout our campus.

    5. Montview Student Union

      The Montview (pronounced "mont-vyoo") Student Union is an expansion of DeMoss Hall focused on leisure and comfort rather than academics. It is a centrally located, modern area where you can hang out with friends, grab a bite to eat, or do homework. The Montview (pronounced "mont-vyoo") features an eight-lane bowling alley and game room, dining options like pizza and pho (pronounced "fuh"), and our Career Services, and Student Government Association offices.

    6. Jerry Falwell Library

      Named in honor of our founder, the Jerry Falwell (pronounced "fall-well") Library has four floors tailored to the different styles of learning, with spaces for collaboration, technology, and deep quiet for focusing. You can find more than 65,000 commonly-used books on the traditional book shelving throughout most of the building, or choose from nearly half a million that are stored in the Automatic Storage Retrieval System, also known as "the robot." There are study rooms with whiteboard walls for brainstorming and collaboration, as well as a Starbucks, Einstein Bros. Bagels, Pizza Hut, and Auntie Anne's for a quick food break.

    7. Center for Music & the Worship Arts

      The Center for Music & the Worship Arts is home to the 7th largest school of music in the country. You could practice in one of our 50 soundproof modules, perform in large and small recital halls, or record in our studio. We are one of just 200 all-Steinway schools in the world, meaning all our pianos are of the highest quality so you can prepare to be the best of the best. The highlight is the 1,600-seat Grand Concert Hall that includes a Meyer (pronounced "my-ur") Sound Constellation Acoustic System, which allows the room acoustics to adjust dependent on the type of music performed.

    8. Center for Natural Science

      Center for Natural Sciences is devoted to providing opportunities for students of both the undergraduate and graduate levels to gain hands-on experience in high tech laboratories. There are more than 20 wet labs, a cadaver lab, a cell culture facility, and more lab spaces, meaning you can grow confident in your practical knowledge of the sciences before entering graduate school or the workforce.

    9. School of Business

      Our School of Business is housed in a new building established entirely to bolster this imperative and growing program. Made up of innovative spaces, information technology labs, networking and data centers, and a stock trading simulation room with ticker boards, you will immerse yourself in an academic environment designed to fully prepare you for your future profession.

    10. Club Sports

      Liberty's Club Sports program is modeled after an NCAA Division III athletics department and is unlike any other in the country. With 40 teams and more than 700 members and coaches, as well as professional grade facilities and training areas, Liberty's Club Sports programs bring serious match-ups and exhilarating competition to the Liberty arena.

    11. Liberty Athletics

      Williams Stadium is home to the Liberty Flames Football team, who play at the NCAA Division I level in the FBS. You will be able to attend games for free as a student and help fill the recently expanded 25,000-seat venue to cheer the Flames on to victory. Williams Stadium also hosts Commencement each year, where speakers such as Vice President Mike Pence, Mel Gibson, and Shannon Bream, have addressed the new alumni.

    12. Food Court at Reber-Thomas Dining Hall

      The Food Court at Reber (pronounced "ree-burr")-Thomas is our primary dining hall with buffet-style dining from more than 20 unique stations such as a Mongolian (pronounced "mong-goh-lee-uhn") barbeque, home-style cooking, an omelet bar, fresh salads, and burritos where you don't pay extra for guacamole. If you need vegan or gluten- and allergen-free options to fit your diet, we've got you covered with delicious and safely-prepared options throughout our dining hall. Liberty's dining has ranked in the top 5 by BestColleges.com for the past four years for a reason!

    13. Freedom Tower

      Home to our John W. Rawlings (pronounced "rah-lings") School of Divinity, the Freedom Tower is the pinnacle of the academic lawn to reflect our commitment to the foundation of Liberty University-our faith in Christ. As the tallest building in the city of Lynchburg, it provides a remarkable view of campus and the surrounding area, as well as contains classrooms, offices, and two observation decks. You'll also find a biblical scriptorium with a collection of rare books and Bibles, including the world's smallest Bible and a replica of the Gutenberg press.

    14. LaHaye Recreation and Fitness Center and Intramural Sports

      Whatever your preferred form of exercise, you will be able to enjoy it at the two-story LaHaye (pronounced "lah-hay") Recreation & Fitness Center. You can try out high quality weightlifting and cardio equipment, a forty-foot rock wall, or the junior size Olympic swimming pool. If group classes are more your thing, you can sign up for cycling, aerobics, TRX, Zumba (pronounced "zoom-buh"), yoga, and more. To gamify your workout, pull a team together for indoor soccer, basketball, or racquetball. And if you're looking for a recreational sports league, choose one (or several!) of our 20+ intramural sports and participate in tournaments throughout the year. This is the perfect place to combat your freshman 15 fears.

    15. Residence Halls

      The Academic Lawn is a place to gather and connect the campus from the north end to the south. Our Student Government Association hosts tailgates for football games in its central area, and you'll regularly find various fairs and activities going on here. Most on-campus housing connects to the lawn, with two of our three dorm styles found along its path. The first is the Residential Commons, hotel-style housing with private bathrooms and common areas on each floor, and the second is the Hill and Circle dorms, which are main campus-style with community bathrooms on a traditional hall. Further from the lawn you'll find apartment-style living with three bedrooms per apartment as well as a full kitchen and living area. Come visit to find which housing option is right for you!

    16. School of Aeronautics

      Our School of Aeronautics is a robust program that will prepare you for a career as a pilot, airline attendant, maintenance technician, or drone operator. You'll learn from talented instructors in state-of-the-art facilities using simulators and actual aircraft. You can be confident that your education will give you the hands-on experience necessary in addition to an understanding of practical theory, all taught from a biblical worldview.
