Members, bodies and activities  

         

705 MEPs

The European Parliament is made up of 705 Members, who are directly elected.

Go to the website : MEPs  

President David Maria SASSOLI

The President is elected for a renewable two and a half years, meaning half a Parliamentary term.

Go to the President's website : The President  

7 Political groups

MEPs sit in political groups. They are not organised by nationality, but by political affiliation.

  1. EPP: Group of the European People's Party (Christian Democrats)
  2. S&D: Group of the Progressive Alliance of Socialists and Democrats in the European Parliament
  3. Renew: Renew Europe
  4. ID: Identity and Democracy
  5. Greens/EFA: The Greens/European Free Alliance
  6. ECR: European Conservatives and Reformists
  7. GUE/NGL: European United Left - Nordic Green Left
Go to the website : Political groups  

Plenary

During plenary sessions MEPs vote on European laws and take position on issues through debates.

Go to the website : Plenary

What is the European Parliament?  

The European Parliament is the EU's only directly-elected institution.
Its 705 Members are there to represent you.
