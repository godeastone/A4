Explore Campus

Duquesne University

  1. Old Main & Chapel

    Welcome to Duquesne University. My name is Ronald Curry, and I'm a member of Duquesne student government association. You will also hear from my friend Aly, an admissions ambassador. Before we get started, let me explain the many ways you can explore our campus, which is a park-like setting on the edge of downtown Pittsburgh. You can click the forward-facing arrow to start walking around. You can jump to the next stop by clicking on the next button. Now, if you already know where you want to go, you can select any location by using the list or by clicking directly on the map. Lastly, remember that you can explore any particular location in more detail by clicking 360, photos, or video icons.

    The Spiritan congregation, known as the Spiritans, founded Duquesne in 1878 as a Catholic institution. We're starting our tour at the Administration Building, also known as Old Main. It is the oldest building on Duquesne's campus, built in 1885. Today, it is home to the offices of undergraduate admissions, financial aid, the registrar, and the tutoring center, which is free for all students in every major. Here, you'll also find the office of the president, along with many other offices overseeing day-to-day operations. Campus Ministry and the Chapel of the Holy Spirit are located here as well.

  2. Bayer Hall - Pharmacy

    Welcome to Bayer Hall, home to the School of Pharmacy and Graduate School of Pharmaceutical Sciences. The school is one of top in the nation among private pharmacy schools for securing funds from the National Institutes of Health. Our largest program is our six-year freshman to PharmD Program, which continues to have a 99% job placement rate. Graduate offerings include an online PharmD program and multidisciplinary Master of Science and PhD programs. The School of Pharmacy also has several academic research centers and programs for hands-on opportunities.

  3. Canevin Hall - Education

    Canevin Hall is home to the School of Education. With national accreditation for the full range of its doctoral, masters, undergraduate and certification programs, students can select from many opportunities that lead to successful careers both inside and outside the classroom. The school's offerings include the leading teacher program where students experience a variety of educational field settings beginning freshman year. The school also offers a diverse portfolio of graduate programs within the departments of educational foundations and leadership, instruction and leadership in education and counseling, psychology and special education.

  4. Student Life

    At Duquesne, you'll become a part of a tight-knit community as you study, work and socialize with fellow students, forming strong bonds and lifelong friendships. Our students are serious about academics and serious about fun. Duquesne offers more than 250 student organizations including recreation and clubs sports, and community service, multicultural, spiritual, professional, and academic groups. There are also a lot of opportunities to attend live concerts, movies, and other special events on campus. Students can often get discounted tickets to the many cultural events and activities close to campus. Duquesne's loop bus and South Side shuttle provide access to many other activities and recreation hotspots.

  5. Mellon Hall - Natural & Environmental Sciences

    The Bayer School of Natural and Environmental Sciences offers bachelors, masters and doctoral programs. It is large enough that you'll have hands-on research opportunities in state-of- the- art labs with cutting edge equipment, yet small enough that you'll work with accomplished faculty who will know you by name. Bayer School graduates are accepted to medical schools at rates more than twice the national average and attend top graduate schools in their field. Corporate, medical and graduate school representatives regularly visit campus to recruit our science students.

  6. Academic Walk

    Academic Walk (we call it A-Walk), is a main thoroughfare through our park-like campus, starting at the Administration Building and ending at the residence halls. It's a place where students can access many academic buildings, the student union and Rooney Field, where you can find anything from a Duquesne Duke's football game to women's lacrosse practice, to an intramural soccer or pickup Frisbee game. Academic Walk is also a great place just to hang out with friends or participate in many student activities and events that take place here throughout the year.

  7. Dining

    Duquesne is not your typical student dining experience. With 11 student dining locations on campus, you can choose from a wide variety of delicious and healthy meals featuring local and sustainable food sources and special options for every diet and taste. Here at Towers Residence Hall, you will find the all-you-care-to eat Hogan Dining Center, our largest dining location, as well as the Campus Market for grab-and-go snacks, salads and sandwiches. We also have Starbucks, Chick-fil-a, Cinco Cantina, Freshens and a full-service restaurant, The Red Ring, right on campus. The Daily Meal recently named Duquesne one of the top 75 best colleges for food in America.

  8. Residence Halls

    Approximately two-thirds of our undergraduate students and many of our graduate students live in one of seven residence halls on campus, ranging from traditional dorm style to suites and apartments. We call them Living Learning Centers because they are much more than dorms. These residence halls are where students get together to study, to work on service projects and to socialize. They are located a short walk from Duquesne's academic buildings, dining and other campus amenities. The Tower Living Learning Center also has a pool and the largest student dining hall on campus. And laundry is free in all of the buildings. You just have to bring your own detergent.

  9. Athletics

    The renovated and reimagined UPMC Cooper Fieldhouse will serve as the new home for athletics. Fifteen of Duquesne's seventeen athletic teams compete in the Atlantic 10 Conference, widely recognized as the top NCAA Division I basketball-only league in the country. You can attend Duquesne's men's and women's hoops games, as well as women's volleyball games. The Duquesne football team, which plays in the Northeast Conference, competes in the NCAA Football Championship Subdivision. Dukes football as well as men's and women's soccer and women's lacrosse all play on Rooney Field, located in the center of campus. Admission for all home events is free, you'll need to show a valid student ID for basketball and football games only. Go Dukes!

  10. Music Building

    The Mary Pappert School of Music has the faculty, resources and a wide variety of undergraduate, graduate and dual degree programs to prepare you for a successful career. We are an all-Steinway school with more than 60 rehearsal and performance spaces, as well as recording studios and music technology labs. Our school is home to two acoustically superb and technologically sophisticated concert venues that are ideal for recital and chamber ensemble performances.

  11. College Hall - Liberal Arts

    The College was Duquesne's first school of study. Today, our students can choose from more than 30 undergraduate and 20 graduate liberal arts degrees programs. It's home to the University's core curriculum, which is the foundation for every undergraduate program at the university. Undergraduate students can participate in MAP, the McAnulty College internship program. It provides every student in the College at least one academic internship opportunity for credit related to his or her major. Our graduates have careers in a variety of sectors, including nonprofits, health care, government and politics, sports, education, law, the religious world, and private industries.

  12. Duquesne Union

    The Union is at the center of campus and provides a number of activities and resources for students. Here, you'll find the popular Nite Spot, offering pool and ping-pong tables, foosball, shuffleboard and a TV/movie room. There's also the Commuter Center where commuters can study, socialize and relax in between classes, and the University Center for Career Development where you can get assistance with the internship and job search processes beginning freshman year. Starbucks, ATMs, a small Barnes & Noble, computing and technology help desk, computer store, student lounge and University Health Service are also located here. There are also a number of offices, including Freshman Development and Disability Services, Diversity and Inclusion, and Study Abroad and International Admissions.

  13. Hanley Hall - Law

    We're at the home of Duquesne's nationally ranked law school. It's a place where students get to know their deans and faculty personally. The law school has moot courtrooms, a legal writing center, study areas and a law library, along with a coffee shop, lounge and offices for student organizations. Law students serve the community while working for credit and receiving practical legal education. Clinical opportunities include civil rights, education law, family law, federal litigation, juvenile defender, unemployment compensation, urban development and veterans clinics. Duquesne Law is located steps from Pittsburgh's legal epicenter, so gaining experience in downtown law firms, courts and businesses is convenient. The university's Pre-Law Center is also located here.

  14. Gumberg Library

    The Gumberg Library is a five-story building with quiet floors, group study areas, and unique special collections, as well as access to printed books, e-books and databases. The Gumberg Library is not only an important resource hub, but also offers students access to technology, resources for class work and free cultural events. The library is run by faculty and staff who can help you find material in the library or around the world, schedule one-on-one consultations to help with research, and teach classes on helpful resources. The library even offers 24/7 hours during finals exams.

  15. Power Center

    The Power Center is another popular place on campus in the recreation center. You'll find cardio, fitness, exercise equipment, basketball and racquetball courts, fitness and dance studios, locker rooms, an indoor track and study lounges. In addition to physical fitness, we also have counseling and wellbeing services on campus. The Charles J. Dougherty Ballroom hosts various speakers and events you can attend, and enjoy the view of the city while you're there. A Barnes and Noble campus bookstore and cafe are located on the ground level of the Power Center, along with Freshens for smoothies and rice bowls, and The Red Ring, a full-service restaurant named after Duquesne's iconic class ring.

  16. Fisher Hall - Nursing

    The School of Nursing is located here in Fisher Hall. The Nursing School's undergraduate and graduate programs are consistently ranked among the nation's best. Known as an innovative leader in the nursing education, the school was the first to offer a biomedical engineering and nursing dual degree and a PhD in nursing and in nursing ethics. Pittsburgh hospitals offer students outstanding opportunities for clinical experiences, internships and professional nursing practice. All levels of the nursing curriculum incorporate ethics, social justice and a global perspective. Fisher Hall also houses several health science labs. You will also find the pre-medical and health professions programs, counseling and well-being center and the physics department's state-of-the-art research and learning labs here.

  17. Libermann Hall - Biomedical Engineering

    Libermann Hall is home to our state-of-the-art biomedical engineering labs. You will also find the School of Nursing's hands-on learning and simulation center, where students have access to the latest learning technology and health care resources. The occupational therapy program also has a lab space here. Other offices in Libermann include graduate admissions, the Paralegal Institute, the Office for Military and Veteran Students and Student Veterans Center.

  18. Rockwell Hall - Business

    We're standing in front of Rockwell Hall, home to the Palumbo Donahue School of Business. Our school of business is an AACSB-accredited school offering bachelors, masters and executive education programs. Our business students have access to cutting-edge facilities, including a makerspace, investment center, as well as other centers of excellence in supply chain management, entrepreneurship and professional selling. Rockwell Hall gives students easy access to downtown, and it houses Duquesne's Small Business Development Center, which is open to the Pittsburgh community, as well as the university psychology clinic.

  19. Health Sciences Building

    The John G. Rangos Senior School of Health Sciences offers best-in-field academic programs—from a new bachelor's degree in public health to accredited professional masters and doctoral programs. The school's nationally and internationally renowned faculty emphasize interprofessional education, evidence-based practice and community-based learning experiences in their teaching and mentorship. Students have ample opportunities to be involved in ongoing research projects and participate in service learning, clinical placement and internships aimed at improving the health and quality of life for people across their lifespan.

  20. Pittsburgh

    Duquesne's campus is just steps away from downtown Pittsburgh and walking distance to internships, recreation shopping and more. Catch a Steelers or Pirates game, or walk across Fifth Avenue for student rush tickets to the Pittsburgh Penguins. Students also enjoy shopping downtown and in several nearby neighborhoods such as the Strip District, Shadyside's Walnut Street, South Side, or one of several nearby malls. If outdoor activities are more your style, you'll enjoy biking and walking trails or kayaking along Pittsburgh's Three Rivers. Pittsburgh is also a foodie town with dozens of restaurants within walking distance of campus.
